How to read this
Territories
Each polygon = the spatial region closest to that company's center. Larger or central cells just mean fewer competitors nearby in the layout — not market share.
Why companies sit where they do
Force-directed layout: companies are pulled toward their vertical's cluster center, repelled by overlap. Position is structural, not geographic.
Contour lines
Topographic isolines = funding-density elevation. Peaks rise where the most-funded companies cluster. Higher altitude = more capital deployed in that region.
Dot size
Dot size = synthesized total funding for that company (deterministic, marked SYN until real data is plumbed in).
Caveat Layout is deterministic but not geographic — Berlin and San Francisco aren't shown by location. Verticals form clusters; that's the structure.
